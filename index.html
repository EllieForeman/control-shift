Control Shift

5-7 June 2020

‘Control Shift’ asks how we can create an alternative vision of technology that combats the abstract computational processes that are pervasive in contemporary culture, and explore possibilities for technology that are based around care, embodied interaction, and critical reflection. Where ‘care’ might be thought of more expansively than its usual framing and might include a more situated, affective and connected approach to technology that includes sociotechnical assemblages and objects as lively politically charged “things.” (de La Bellacasa, 2017) It might also echo some of Latour’s “matters of concern” and Dingpolitik.
