Power Tools by Ben and Arjun:

Who we are

We are a London-based artist & designer duo exploring alternative forms of Accessibility in relation to Disability Rights in the UK. Our work is grounded in the Social Model of Disability — the belief that people aren’t disabled, but society is disabling.

What we do

We make physical and digital tools that respond to various communication and access needs. Our work combines and subverts existing communications technologies, video-calling formats and wearable accessories.

This current project

We will programme a series of 3 paired walks. Each pairing will use our ‘Power tools’ to have remote discussions around the Social Model of Disability, exploring issues around: power & presence, access & inclusion.

The walk and accompanying conversations will take place during the outdoor activity currently allowed, as part of lockdown measures — although still inaccessible to many. We are interested to explore what impact the current global health crisis may be having on peoples understanding of and empathy with the idea that ‘people aren’t disabled, but society is disabling’.

Opening up these discussions as a resource for wider audiences, we hope to encourage others to consider the importance of accessibility in their lives and what they can do to engage with the systems and structures that help and hinder them.

Knitwitter by Wisterlitz:

Who we are

Wisterlitz is the joint enterprise of Liz Lister and Rebecca (Becky) Hurwitz. We make playful, whimsical, interactive work that aims to engage people in the relationships between science/tech and society. Our work often reflects on the position of women in relation to these areas.

What we do

Our creative endeavours revolve around the process of making – defined as an iterative process of tinkering and problem solving – done in collaboration. Together, we learn how materials respond as we use them and what tools can do as we try to work with them.

This current project

Women have used knitting to send secret messages since WW1. Seen a woman helpfully knitting socks for the troops? Maybe she’s a spy encoding secret messages into her knitting, hiding in plain sight. Nowadays, we all send encoded messages all the time. Our instant, digital, text-based, communications are ultimately made up of millions of hidden away zeros and ones that we entrust our phones to interpret for us and keep secret from others. What if we could send our secret messages not as text on a screen, but as an everyday item, something innocuous, hiding in plain sight?

Participate

You can do just this by using the online Knitwitter binary encoding tools and your knitting skills to hide a message in a piece of knitting, then send your knitting to us. Wisterlitz will then collect, curate and display all the knitted messages we receive. Knitwitter will be a collaborative artwork that creates tactile engagement with digital data. It draws on our intimate yet intangible relationship with the data we generate through personal communications in the digital world, and anyone with knitting needles and yarn can contribute

Wisterlitz gratefully acknowledges all contributions, but we won’t be decoding your messages. That data will stay hidden in the knitting.
